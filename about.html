About Me

I am a Healer. I am a Social Worker. I am a Yoga Teacher. I use my experiences and training to guide others on the path to be balanced and heal if necessary and to be healthy on all levels of their being. I integrate therapeutic modalities with the movement and breathing of yoga poses, along with guidance about our spiritual bodies to improve our awareness of and attunement to ourselves. I use my skills particularly to address the psychological consequences of gender based violence and other traumatic experiences. My yoga training and teaching focuses on the experience of yoga to develop awareness of the self through moving meditation. The focus is on interception; noticing what it feels like to live in this incredible entity of the physical human body. By being present to the internal sensations you can connect to this material aspect of your being in a deeper way. By making choices about your movements, you will learn how to make more mindful decisions for your body off the mat as well. Traumatic experiences sometimes lead one to disconnect from their body, through this conscious yoga experience you will discover that your body is a safe and special place to be. Through my guidance you will begin to identify the emotions which are stored in your muscles and learn to process them. By learning to breathe intentionally during a class, you will strengthen your ability to control your emotions through your nervous system. As we go through a yoga asana, the energy of our being also moves around, and we will notice this through reviewing the chakra system. By becoming aware of the physical, emotional and mental aspects of your self, you can make progress towards letting go of that which no longer serves you and attending to that which you want to experience more of. My style of conscious yoga is a spiritual practice through physical movements to connect to the self and to spirit.

My Mission

My mission is to provide people with an experiential form of learning about themselves in order to transform mind, body and spirit. I aim to help survivors of traumatic events to cope through yoga, therapy and spiritual empowerment.
